What is the I3E?

The IESE Economic Uncertainty Index (I3E) is a daily indicator of economic uncertainty in the largest world economies. The index is computed daily, using the variation rates of 4 variables in each country:

  • Domestic Stock Index
  • Domestic 10-year government bond yields
  • Domestic exchange rate
  • International Brent crude oil prices

The index has an average value of 100. Although it can vary across any range, in practice it fluctuates between 0 and 200. Values below 100 indicate uncertainty lower than the average of previous decades. Values above 100 indicate greater uncertainty.

The objective of the I3E is to provide a quantifiable and bias-free measure of economic uncertainty that can be used by researchers and decision-makers worldwide.
