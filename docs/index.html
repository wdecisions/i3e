What is the I3E?

The IESE Economic Uncertainty Index (I3E) is a daily indicator measuring economic uncertainty in some of the largest world economies. To construct this index, we use the daily variation rates of the following variables in each of the covered countries:

  • Domestic Equity Index
  • Domestic 10-year government bond prices
  • Domestic exchange rate
  • International Brent crude oil prices

The index reflects the magnitude of the daily growth rate fluctuations of these four variables. It is adjusted so that its average value is 100. Although, in theory, the index can vary across any range, in practice its value fluctuates between 0 and 200. Values below 100 indicate economic uncertainty lower than the average of the previous decade, while values above 100 indicate greater economic uncertainty.

Many business and personal decisions, both short- and long-term, are based on prevailing economic uncertainty. The objective of the I3E is to provide a quantifiable measure of this uncertainty that enables decision-making based on information free from subjective bias.
